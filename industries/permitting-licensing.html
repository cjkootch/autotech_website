Permitting & Licensing FAQs

How much time and cost can digital permitting systems save? +

Digital permitting systems reduce application processing time by 40-60% by eliminating manual data entry, automating routing, and enabling parallel processing. Staff productivity increases by 25-35% through workflow automation. Administrative overhead drops by 30-40% from reduced phone inquiries and manual status tracking. These improvements typically generate sufficient cost savings to fully fund system implementation within 18-24 months.

How do online application portals improve citizen experience and usage? +

Online portals enable 24/7 application submission without visiting government offices, provide real-time status updates, allow document uploads and feedback online, and reduce application errors through guided workflows. Usage of online applications typically grows from 20-30% to 60-75% within 18 months of launch. Applicants save hours of time waiting in offices and tracking application status through phone calls.

What data and analytics can permitting systems provide to operations managers? +

Permitting systems capture data on application volume, processing times by application type, staff productivity, inspection scheduling patterns, fee collection, and bottleneck locations. Analytics dashboards provide visibility into queue depth, average processing time, compliance metrics, and revenue trends. This data enables managers to identify process improvements, allocate staff more efficiently, and optimize fee structures based on processing costs.

How do you manage multiple application types with different requirements and review processes? +

Systems support configurable application types with unique forms, required documents, fee schedules, and approval workflows. Each application type can have specific review requirements, inspections, and document needs. Staff are presented with application-specific checklists and requirements. This flexibility allows a single system to handle building permits, business licenses, sign permits, variance requests, and hundreds of other application types with different processes.

How are security, privacy, and compliance requirements addressed in permitting systems? +

Permitting systems maintain secure access controls ensuring applicants only access their own applications, staff access only applications within their jurisdiction, and sensitive data is encrypted. Audit trails capture all actions taken on applications. Systems support public records access requirements while protecting personal information. Compliance with open records laws and data privacy regulations is built into system design and operation.
