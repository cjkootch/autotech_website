Frequently Asked Questions

How do you ensure PIPEDA compliance and data privacy in government solutions?
⌄

PIPEDA compliance is built into our solution architecture from design through implementation. We incorporate data minimization principles, implement robust access controls, establish clear data retention policies, create audit trails for accountability, and provide transparent privacy mechanisms. Our solutions support government's obligations for citizen consent, breach notification, and data portability. We also address provincial privacy laws (like Quebec's Law 25) that may be more stringent than PIPEDA.

What's involved in making systems truly bilingual (English and French)?
⌄

True bilingualism goes beyond simple translation. We design systems with dual-language support built in from the database layer through user interface, ensuring both languages have equal functionality, performance, and user experience. This includes proper character encoding, right-to-left support if needed, language-specific formatting (dates, currency), cultural appropriateness, and professional translation by native speakers. Our systems allow citizens to seamlessly switch languages and maintain their language preference across sessions.

How do you manage federal-provincial-municipal coordination?
⌄

We design systems with clear data governance and sharing frameworks that respect constitutional divisions of power while enabling coordination where needed. Our solutions use secure APIs, audit trails, and consent mechanisms to support appropriate information sharing. We understand the unique dynamics of each jurisdiction and help establish governance frameworks that clarify roles, authorities, and data sharing protocols while maintaining system independence.

What accessibility standards must Canadian government systems meet?
⌄

Requirements vary by jurisdiction. Ontario's AODA sets standards, but other provinces have similar accessibility requirements (e.g., British Columbia, Alberta). Federal systems must meet ATIA standards. We design solutions meeting WCAG 2.1 AA standards as a baseline, implement proper semantic HTML, support assistive technologies, provide alternative text, ensure keyboard navigation, and use appropriate color contrast. We conduct accessibility audits and provide remediation guidance.

How do you handle procurement and funding across government jurisdictions?
⌄

We understand federal procurement (GETS), provincial frameworks, and municipal processes. We're experienced with Treasury Board requirements, provincial government procurement policies, and municipal contracting. We can structure solutions to work with existing vendor relationships, leverage approved procurement channels, align with multi-year budget cycles, and support grant funding requirements. Our approach accommodates the unique fiscal and procurement landscape of different Canadian jurisdictions.
