Fuel Logistics Tracking System

Optimize regional fuel distribution with real-time GPS fleet tracking, automated delivery scheduling, terminal inventory management, and driver mobile app integration for complete operational visibility.

The Scenario

A regional fuel distributor operates 40+ delivery vehicles serving 500+ retail stations across a three-state area. Currently, dispatchers coordinate deliveries using manual scheduling, drivers lack real-time navigation and delivery info, terminals don't track inventory automatically, and management has no visibility into fleet efficiency, fuel consumption patterns, or delivery performance. The company loses time and revenue to inefficient routing, unplanned downtime, and customer service issues from late deliveries.

The Challenge

Fleet Operations Pain Points

Dispatchers spend hours manually planning routes without real-time traffic data. Drivers navigate using outdated maps, face unexpected delays, and can't update customers on ETA. Vehicle maintenance is reactive instead of predictive, and fuel consumption metrics aren't tracked.

Inventory & Asset Visibility

Terminal tank levels are estimated visually instead of measured accurately. Stockouts happen without warning, overstocking ties up capital, and no data exists on product movement patterns to optimize distribution. Customer requests for delivery windows can't be reliably committed to.

  • Route planning takes 1-2 hours per day with poor optimization for traffic and vehicle capacity
  • Delivery times average 15-20% longer than optimal due to inefficient routing and traffic inefficiency
  • No real-time visibility into vehicle location, status, or ETA from dispatch or customer perspective
  • Drivers lack delivery-specific information (product type, volume, special handling requirements)
  • Terminal inventory managed manually with no automated tracking or predictive reordering
  • Customer service issues from missed delivery windows and inability to provide accurate ETAs

We deliver an integrated fleet logistics platform combining real-time GPS tracking, intelligent route optimization, automated inventory management, and mobile driver tools to maximize efficiency, safety, and customer satisfaction.

System Architecture

Mobile & Dispatch Layer
Driver App & Dispatcher Console

Native mobile app (iOS/Android) for drivers with offline-first capability, real-time push notifications, and background GPS tracking. Dispatcher web console for route assignment, live fleet tracking, customer communication, and alert management. Both interfaces sync with the backend in real-time.

Logistics Engine
Route Optimization & Fleet Management

AI-powered route optimizer using historical traffic data and real-time congestion, vehicle constraint modeling (capacity, fuel type), delivery window scheduling, and multi-objective optimization (time, fuel, distance). Fleet management handles driver assignments, performance scoring, maintenance scheduling, and duty hour tracking.

Data Layer
Tracking & Inventory Management

Time-series database for GPS tracking points, vehicle telemetry, and inventory measurements streamed from IoT sensors. PostgreSQL for relational data (routes, deliveries, customers, vehicles, terminals). Redis for caching vehicle state and real-time updates.

Integration Layer
External Systems & IoT

IoT sensor integration with tank monitoring devices. Webhook subscriptions for vehicle telematics (OBD data, GPS). API integrations with customer systems for ETA updates. Email/SMS for customer notifications and driver communications.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the route optimizer account for real-time traffic and driver preferences?
▼
The optimizer uses real-time traffic data from mapping services (Google Maps, HERE) and historical traffic patterns for your region. It can be configured with constraints like vehicle capacity, fuel type compatibility, special handling requirements, and driver availability. You can set multi-objective optimization to balance time, fuel cost, and distance based on your priorities.
What happens if a driver goes offline or loses GPS connectivity?
▼
The mobile app works offline with cached navigation data and delivery information. GPS points are queued and transmitted when connectivity returns. Drivers can complete deliveries offline with photo confirmation and signatures saved locally. The dispatcher console shows last-known location and syncs when connection resumes.
How do IoT tank sensors integrate with the inventory system?
▼
Wireless IoT sensors installed on terminal tanks transmit level readings at configurable intervals (typically 15 minutes). The system correlates consumption with deliveries to calculate burn rates. Predictive algorithms forecast stockout dates based on demand patterns, triggering reorder workflows. Historical data enables optimization of terminal stock levels by location.
Can the system communicate ETAs to customers automatically?
▼
Yes. Customers receive SMS or email notifications with ETA windows whenever a delivery is dispatched. The system updates notifications periodically as the driver approaches (within 30 min, 15 min, 5 min windows). For high-value accounts, a customer portal shows real-time driver location and allows direct communication with the dispatcher.
What kind of driver behavior metrics does the system track?
▼
Metrics include harsh braking events, speeding incidents, excessive idling, sharp turns, and fuel efficiency per driver. The system calculates a safety score for each driver and generates coaching reports highlighting improvement areas. Gamification features (leaderboards, incentives) can encourage safe driving habits and cost-conscious behavior.
